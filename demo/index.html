Mechanics, RTP, and what “crash-style” means

The essence of the game is simple: the multiplier climbs as the chicken advances, and your job is to exit before a hidden hazard ends the run. Because the multiplier rises continuously, every second you hesitate can either boost your return or erase your stake. The game’s RTP is 98%, which signals a mathematically friendly profile across long play, while individual sessions still swing with luck. Stakes are flexible—from as little as INR 0.01 up to INR 200 per round—so you can scale risk to your comfort. Across all modes, outcomes are fast, transparent, and designed to reward timely decisions rather than button-mashing. Cash-out timing and multiplier control

Because the road layout grows riskier, your target multipliers should adjust too: shoot lower on Hardcore than you would on Easy. Frequently Asked Questions

Is the 98% RTP a guarantee I’ll profit?

RTP describes the mathematical return over a very long span of play, not a promise for any short session. In a handful of rounds, results can swing higher or lower than that figure. Your exits, stake size, and mode choice influence how rough those swings feel. Think of RTP as the backdrop; your discipline determines the foreground. Plan your exits, size modestly, and let time do its part.

Which mode should a newcomer start with?

Start on Easy to internalize how fast the multiplier rises and how it feels to exit on time. Once your timing is consistent, step into Medium for added pressure without overwhelming risk. If you ever feel rushed, drop back to Easy and rebuild rhythm. Remember that skill here is mostly about reaction and restraint, not guessing. A steady foundation makes later jumps feel natural.

How do I set a good cash-out target?

Pick a narrow band that fits your mode: wider on Easy, tighter on Hardcore. The band should feel reachable in most rounds so you’re cashing out regularly, not waiting for rare spikes. If you constantly exit too early or too late, adjust the band by small increments and test again. Consistency beats hero moments in the long run. Treat the band like a rule, not a suggestion.

What’s the best way to avoid tilt after a bad streak?

First, pause—short breaks reset timing and emotions. Second, reduce stakes for a few rounds and return to your original exit band; this shrinks risk while you regain flow. Third, end the session if decisions feel impulsive, then review later with a clear head. Tilt fades when you re-assert structure. Your future rounds will thank you.

Can I enjoy the game on a slow connection?

Yes, but keep sessions simple and targets modest. A lighter visual setup and avoiding background apps can improve responsiveness. When latency feels noticeable, prefer lower-risk modes so missed taps don’t hurt as much. Shorter sessions also help when conditions are unstable. The goal is smooth timing, not fighting your network.
